About Me

TAN Hong Ming is the Deputy Head and Senior Lecturer in the Department of Analytics and Operations at the NUS Business School, National University of Singapore. In this capacity, he oversees departmental administration while teaching and coordinating courses across multiple programs, including the Undergraduate, MBA, Executive MBA, MSc in Business Analytics (MSBA), and MSc in Precision Health programs. His contributions also extend to executive education, where he leads courses in AI such as AI for Leaders: Leveraging Data Analytics for Business, and Practical Decision Making Using Data Science.

Beyond teaching, Hong Ming is deeply involved in industry research, serving as the Principal Investigator for the project "Optimising Maritime Network Line Design using Reinforcement Learning" and co-leading initiatives such as the AI-led Smart Data Centre Management Application. He also contributes to the SIA Corp Lab and provides strategic AI leadership as the Lead AI Advisor for X0PA AI and Nexushomes. In all of these roles, he oversees and directs teams of data scientists, PhD students, research assistants, and interns, guiding them through various cutting-edge AI projects and research initiatives in collaboration with industry partners.

Hong Ming serves as the Chair of the Department Finance Committee and sits on the Department Teaching Committee. Additionally, he advises student-led organizations, including the Business Analytics Consulting Team and the NUS Private Equity Society.

Research

As a researcher, my work spans multiple disciplines, including operations, education, aviation, and mental health, where I apply AI to solve real-world challenges. These projects highlight my expertise in utilizing complex statistical methods and data-driven approaches to address pressing business and societal problems, with a strong focus on leveraging technology to tackle real issues.

A significant aspect of my research also involves economic and statistical modeling. I am a firm believer in the power of simple models to explain complex real-world phenomena. By distilling intricate systems into their core components, these models offer valuable insights that inform decision-making and strategy development.

In summary, my research is defined by its interdisciplinary reach and practical applications through industry collaboration, highlighting my focus on using data analytics and simplified models to address real-world problems effectively.
