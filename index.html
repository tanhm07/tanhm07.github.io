About Me

TAN Hong Ming is a Lecturer at the Department of Analytics and Operations, NUS Business School
, National University of Singapore. He is also a Research Fellow at the Institute of Operations Research and Analytics at the same university. In his current role, Hong Ming is responsible for teaching and coordinating modules in the Undergraduate, MBA, Executive MBA, and Master of Science in Business Analytics (MSBA) programs. He is also involved in executive education programs such as Leading with Big Data Analytics and Machine Learning, and Practical Decision Making Using Data Science.

Apart from teaching, Hong Ming is also involved in industrial research projects such as the AI-led Smart Data Centre Management Application where he is co-PI and the SIA Corp Lab. He is the Lead AI Advisor for X0PA AI. He manages teams of Data Scientists, PhD students, research assistants, and interns, guiding and providing direction for the various research projects.

He serves as the Chair of the Department Finance Committee and a member of the Department Teaching Committee, and advisor for the student led clubs Business Analytics Consulting Team and NUS Private Equity Society.

Research

As a researcher, my work spans across multiple disciplines including healthcare, education, aviation, and mental health. These projects demonstrate my ability to apply complex statistical methods and data-driven approaches to real-world business problems, as well as my commitment to leveraging technology to address health issues.

Another significant part of my research involves economic and statistical modeling. I believe in the power of simple models to understand complex real-world phenomena. By distilling complex systems into their essential components, these models provide valuable insights that guide decision-making and strategy.

In summary, my research is characterized by its diversity and practical application, demonstrating my commitment to using data analytics and simple models to solve real-world problems.
